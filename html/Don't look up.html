INTRODUCTION

About this movie


"Astronomy graduate student Kate DiBiaschi (Jennifer Lawrence) and her professor Dr. Randall Mindy (Leonardo DiCaprio) have made a startling discovery: a comet orbiting in our solar system. The problem: the comet will collide directly with Earth. And the other problem is that no one really seems to care. After all, it is hardly convincing to warn mankind that an Everest is about to crash into the Earth and destroy the world. With only six months to go before the comet hits the Earth, they're fighting tooth and nail to keep the news rolling on the news channels 24/7 and get the attention of the cell phone-obsessed masses to raise the world's head a little!


About motivation


The writer-director said that he initially wanted to make a movie about climate change, the epidemic happened, and the light reality was magical enough, so he changed his mind. The film, through a surreal story of planetary crisis, exposes the state of mind of different groups of people from top to bottom of American society in the face of life and death crisis, most of whom are obsessed and incomprehensible. The spicy and satirical caricature of a group of clowns shows the refraction and association of the current warning.

MAIN ROLE INTRODUCTION

Randall Mindy is played by Leonardo DiCaprio. Randall Mindy in the play is a doctor who studies astronomy, Kate Dibiasekai's professor. One day, his students discovered a comet is about to hit the Earth, so after some calculations, he found that the comet will hit the Earth 100%.

Kate DiBiasekai is played by Jennifer Lawrence. Kate in the play is a doctoral candidate in astronomy at Michigan State University who first discovers a 9 km diameter comet about to hit the Earth with the energy of a billion Hiroshima bombs. She told the president about it and also went on a TV show, but no one took the news seriously.
