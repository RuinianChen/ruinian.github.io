About Evaluation of the film


The essence of Marvin's Room is a confrontation between the two sisters, which is especially dialogue-heavy because the film is adapted from a play. Meryl Streep and Diane Keaton work well together to make the otherwise lengthy lines work, and the audience gets to know why they didn't talk to each other, how they feel about putting their father in a nursing home, and how they feel about "psychotic" children. The theme of the film also emerges here: the sister gave up her life for her father, but was lucky enough to love her family so thoroughly.
