About reality


Once, director Martin Scorsese went to Paramount's film library to look for something and overlooked the script material for Forbidden Island. After returning Martin Scorsese got the idea to make the film, and then Scorsese invited Leonardo to star in the film. Scorsese was very faithful to the original, and the film's nagging explanatory dialogue was taken from the original, with only some fantasy passages cut and altered, and some specially designed passages added by director Martin Scorsese.


Most of the entire film was shot on location at the Medfield Asylum in Massachusetts. On several occasions during the shoot, Martin Scorsese had to change the shooting schedule due to the weather. The crew had to choose locations based on weather forecasts and satellites before each shoot, but bad weather was still inevitable. At times the weather was a major problem for the actors, as the film has a large number of location shots, but the actors managed to get the job done.
