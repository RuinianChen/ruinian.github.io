About this movie


The Massachusetts underworld in southern Boston is rampant, and the biggest gang is the Irish gang run by Frank (Jack Nicholson). In order to eliminate them, the police decide to send police academy graduate Billy (Leonardo DiCaprio) to lurk undercover. The sneering irony of the police officer made Billy, who had a life at the bottom, very unhappy, he had hoped to be a dignified policeman, but under strong pressure, he had to obey orders, and after the baptism of burning and looting, he won Frank's trust. Meanwhile, Colin (Matt Damon), a fellow graduate, has been awarded by his superiors for his private exploits and is preparing for a marriage with his beloved girlfriend. In fact, he was a pawn that Frank had planted in the police force, and the two colluded for personal gain. The police and bandits have discovered the existence of internal ghosts, and a cat-and-mouse game has begun...The film won Best Picture at the 79th Academy Awards and Martin Scorsese won Best Director.


About reality


The technical advisor for the film was retired Detective Thomas B. D., a 30-year veteran of the Boston Police Department.
Initially, Jack Nicholson was reluctant to do the film, but after meeting Martin Scorsese and Leonardo dicaprio, he finally decided to play Frank, because he had been in a series of comedies, he wanted to play the bad guy again, and he thought Frank was the ultimate incarnation of evil.
