About this movie


"The movie "The Great Gatsby" is a romantic drama film directed by Baz Luhrmann, starring Leonardo DiCaprio, Tobey Maguire, Carey Mulligan, Joel Edgerton, Ella Fischer and Jason Clarke in the lead roles. The film is based on Fitzgerald's novel of the same name and was officially released in the U.S. on May 10, 2013.


About plot


The film tells the story of Nick Carraway, an unsung writer who is so captivated by the world of high society and the fantasies, loves and lies within it, that he witnesses everything inside and outside of this world and decides to write a story about an unrequited love, undying dreams and heartbreaking stories that reflect the current times and struggles. Nick, the unsung writer, came to New York from his Midwestern hometown, where he lived next to Gatsby's lavish mansion. There was a big party going on every night. Nick and Gatsby met and were intrigued by Gatsby. The result: Nick learns that Gatsby has a deep-seated love affair. As a young man, Gatsby was not rich, he was a major officer. He fell in love with a girl named Daisy, who also had a crush on him. Then the First World War broke out and Gatsby was transferred to Europe. Seemingly by chance but also by necessity, Daisy and he broke up, and turned to a rich family from the playboy Tom married. Daisy's life after marriage is not happy, because Tom has another mistress. Material satisfaction does not fill Daisy's spiritual emptiness. Gatsby was so distressed that he was convinced that it was money that made Daisy betray her spiritual chastity, so he aspired to become a rich man. A few years later, Gatsby finally succeeded. He built a mansion across the street from Daisy's house. Gatsby spends money like money, all night long, bent on attracting Daisy's attention to regain the lost love.
Nick is touched by Gatsby's infatuation and goes to visit his distant cousin Daisy, whom he has not contacted for a long time, and conveys Gatsby's feelings to her. Daisy is intentionally flirting with Gatsby at times during their meeting. Gatsby listened to her at will, and naively thought that the unrequited love had the desired ending. However, the real tragedy is at this point quietly started.
