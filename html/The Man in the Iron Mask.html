INTRODUCTION

About this movie


After the death of King Louis XIII of France in 1643, He was succeeded by an arrogant, cruel and immoral Louis XIV. In order to take possession of Kirsty, Louis sent Leo, son of Athas, a former member of the King's guard, to the front. The death of Leo aroused arthas's grief and anger. Meanwhile, Father Elamis has a secret plan to rescue the mysterious prisoner wearing the iron mask from the prison, and try to get him to replace Louis and save the French people in the fire. And this mysterious prisoner is Louis's twin brother Philip. Elameth's plan was supported by arthas and Bausas, both royal guards. However, the current captain of the Royal Guard, Datana, was loyal to King Louis XIV. After elameth managed to rescue Philip, she prepared to reclaim the throne for Him at the royal masquerade Ball. However, Elameth's actions were detected by datana and pursued. Outnumbered, the three Elamethas allowed Philip to fall back into Louis' hands. Louis decides to put Philip back on death row while ordering Datana to kill Elamis. After learning that Philip was his son with Queen Anna, Datana decided to help Elameth rescue Philip. After a bloody battle, Philip finally regained the throne from Louis.


About behind the scenes


In 1997, MGM boss Lindsey Duran, inspired by historical films such as Braveheart, pulled the 1929 version from her studio's library and decided to make a period film based on it. The investors tapped "Braveheart" screenwriter Randall Wallace to direct the film. In April 1997, Randall Wallace took the crew to Apalon Studios outside Paris to shoot the film.
