INTRODUCTION

About this movie


"The movie "The Wolf of Wall Street" is a comedy film directed by Martin Scorsese, starring Leonardo DiCaprio, Margot Robbie, and Jonah Hill in the lead roles. The film tells the story of Wall Street legend Jordan Belfort, a stockbroker who once earned $12 million in three minutes and had a billion dollar fortune at the age of 31. Based on Jordan's personal memoirs, the film tells the story of his fortune as he wandered the fringes of the law and his sunken life lost to sex and drugs.


About reality


This movie is based on a real case. Jordan Belfort, a legendary stockbroker in the 1980s, was nicknamed the "Wolf of Wall Street" by Forbes. At the peak of his firm, Stratton Oakmont employed 1,000 stockbrokers, involving a total of over $1 billion in stock offerings. He never hired experienced brokers, but recruited his own friends and family along with twenty-somethings to follow his ruthless sales credo: sell or die. He was later arrested for fraud.

MAIN ROLE INTRODUCTION

Jordan Belfort, played by Leonardo dicaprio, He is a capitalist with guts and strategy, and lost in the drinking game of Wall Street. He lives for money, dirty tricks, parties, and beauty.

Jean-Jacques Suriel is played by actor Jean Dujardin. He is a genteel Swiss banker who exploits legal loopholes to launder money for the Americans, making gray deals and negotiating around Jordan in the play.
