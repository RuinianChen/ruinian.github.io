INTRODUCTION

About this movie


In 1870, at the age of 16, Rimbaud sent a collection of his poems to the iconic French poet Verlaine. Verlaine replied: "Come here, my poet." Every time rimbaud discussed poetry, his speech was always radical and avant-garde, verlaine was soon won over by Rimbaud's talent. When two people get to know each other, gradually become inseparable, the relationship also develops by each other's mutual appreciation to burst out the emotional light of additional kind.

So the two poets were united. Verlaine runs away and begins a free trip with Rimbaud. They frolic in the wild and watch the sunset together. Rimbaud, seeing the sea for the first time, was so excited that the two held each other tightly. They lived a humble life in London. Rimbaud once again calls Verlaine a bitch at the window, and verlaine leaves. Facing Verlaine as he boarded the boat, Rimbaud cried and said he was sorry, but it was too late. The relationship between the two fell apart after a subsequent meeting. Verlaine hesitated on the edge of flight, but Rimbaud made his choice and prepared to leave verlaine. In his pride verlaine shot Rimbaud through the palm. Their wild love affair was exposed and Verlaine was sentenced to prison. After Verlaine was released from prison, the two climbed mountains together again. But rimbaud had already decided to call it quits. He left Verlaine, who still loved him, and went to Africa alone. He returned to France with a deadly tumor on his knee and died in silence in Marseilles. Verlaine looked sad when he heard of the death. In front of him sat Rimbaud, who had made his heart ache, caressing his hand.

MAIN ROLE INTRODUCTION

Rimbaud was a talented young man from the country. He came to Paris at the invitation of Verlaine and lived with the Verlaine family. Rimbaud is arrogant, eccentric and unruly, causing disgust and fear from others except Verlaine, and conflict with Verlaine's father-in-law. But Verlaine fell for him, and they moved in together. When Rimbaud tried to leave Verlaine, verlaine beat him in the hand.

Verlaine was a French poet of some renown at the time. After meeting Rimbaud, he appreciates his talent and falls in love with rimbaud. However, under the social atmosphere at that time, their love was not accepted by the orthodox society. Verlaine later abandoned his wife and children for Rimbaud, and they went to Belgium and England to stray. Later in the face of preparing to leave their Own Rambo, Verlaine pride can not accept, shot and wounded Rambo, and therefore in prison. Finally, after learning rimbaud's death, I miss Rimbaud alone.
